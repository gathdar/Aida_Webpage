Synopsis of Verdi's Aida

ACT I

The opera opens into a dialogue between the High Priest, Ramfis, and a young captain, Radames.
Radames is told that the Goddess Isis will soon choose the warrior who will lead Egypt's people to victory.

Radames sings "Celeste Aida," hoping to be chosen to lead the army. He wishes to set his lover Aida, who is a servant of the princess Amneris, free.

Aida and Amneris enter, and while Radames and Aida pretend there is nothing between them, Amneris suspects them. Amneris herself is infatuated with Radames and grows angry at the thought.

Next, a great crowd is present at the announcement of the chosen general. It is revealed to be Radames, who thanks the gods.

The crowd sings "Ritorna vincitor," or "Return as a victor." Aida gets caught up in the moment and in her feelings for Radames and sings with them.

As a citizen of Ethiopia, the country against which her lover will fight, she sings of her conflicting feelings.

Act I ends with a powerful ceremony preparing Radames for battle.


ACT II

Act II begins in Amneris' chamber, where she tricks Aida into divulging her feelings for Radames.

The famous triumphal march is next, as the victorious Radames brings the prisoners and spoils of war to the Pharoah.

Aida's father, the disguised king Amonasro, is among the prisoners. They plead for clemency.

Radames asks for the prisoners to be released, and the Pharoah grants his request, keeping only Aida and her father to secure the peace.


ACT III

Act III begins with Aida lamenting her cruel fate, as she is certain she will never see her homeland again.

After being manipulated by her father, Aida is joined by Radames, who unwittingly reveals crucial military secrets.

Triumphant, Amonasro leaps out of the bushes and reveals himself as the king. Radames is bewildered and chagrined. "I am dishonored. For you I have betrayed my country!"

Amneris and Ramfis discover the trio. Aida and her father flee and Radames is left to face the wrath of justice.


ACT IV

At the beginning of Act IV Radames is brought to Amneris, who offers him freedom if he marries her. He refuses. "I fear not the wrath of man; I fear only your pity."

He is found guilty and will be buried alive.

In the tomb he discovers Aida, who heard of his plight and crept into his burial place. They sing a farewell to the earth, while Amneris prays for peace above them.
